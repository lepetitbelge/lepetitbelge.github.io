About me

I was born and raised in Belgium, by a Belgian father and a British mother. I have studied or worked in Belgium, the UK, the Netherlands & Israel. And now I am living in Paris. Living in these different countries has widened my experiences and allowed me to make friends all over Europe. As a result I am fluent in more than one language (including digital ones).

My educational roots lie in Economics (KU Leuven) and Psychology (University of Edinburgh). I combined that knowledge in a small consultancy company in the Netherlands on projects including gamification, data analysis and agile ways of working.

The next challenge for me lies in transfering these skills to the fun and exciting world of coding. In a humble capacity I hope to bridge the gap between this world and the real world wherever possible. My philosophy on tech is that it should complement us, not substitute us.

The coding journey began just a few months ago, which probably means the best is yet to come... Yala, let's code.

Speechless

At the end of an intensive 9-week Le Wagon bootcamp, students were challenged to form teams and create an audacious project of their own. Speechless.world is the result of that effort.

The aim of Speechless is to facilitate a meaningful discussion on topics that are relevant to us, based on the words of our politicians and leaders. Speechless displays the transcripts of speeches, on which comments can be made, votes can be given and discussions can be held.

This pitch showcases the app and the meaning behind it.

Moviemapper

This little project is very much in its MVP phase. I develop moviemapper just for fun in my spare time. It's a playground for practicing with API's, testing and vanilla JS.

On MovieMapper you can search for movies and discover all of its filming locations on a nice and neat map.

The ambition is to create a great map viewing experience, including: trailers, photo's and descriptions per location, a movie bio and much more. I am very open to feedback, the more critical the better. So check it out and let me know what's missing :)
